RADAR 2.0: Enterprise Diagnostics

The Project: A global Remote Analysis and Diagnosis tool for service technicians to troubleshoot complex medical systems.

My Role: UX Design Lead. Conducted global user research, engineered Information Architecture, and provided exact front/back-end specifications.

The Goal: Modernize a decade-old legacy platform to reduce turnaround times and lower severe company overhead costs.

02 / 10

The Enterprise Challenge

  • Reluctance to Change: Global users were deeply entrenched in a legacy mental model.
  • Inefficiency: Highly unoptimized workflows resulted in expensive, unnecessary field visits by technicians.
  • The Scope: The business required a massive platform upgrade, not just a superficial UI reskin.

03 / 10

Methodology: Beyond the Screen

Semi-Structured Interviews: Engaged users across the Americas, Europe, India, and China, treating them as domain experts to uncover motivations and distress points.

Fly-on-the-Wall Shadowing: Monitored actual usage to bridge the gap between what users reported and how they actually bypassed the legacy interface.

The Tells: I actively looked for sighs, eye rolls, and undocumented workarounds to find the true friction points.
