Overview

Project overview

This case study covers the redesign of a mobile wealth-management experience for HSBC, focusing on an Insights & Knowledge Center that educates retail investors and a Customer Dashboard that turns numbers into clear next steps.

The goal was to move away from static content and dense dashboards toward a guided, personalized experience that supports both novice and experienced investors without eroding trust.

The challenge

Retail investors increasingly manage their finances through mobile apps, yet three issues kept surfacing across competitor reviews and stakeholder interviews:

  • Siloed learning between educational content and real investment flows.
  • Number overload on dashboards with no obvious “what should I do now?”.
  • Gamification distrust when features felt promotional instead of educational.

Users

Who we designed for

Research and existing financial literacy studies surfaced two primary user groups that framed both content and dashboard decisions.

Segment 01
Emerging investors

Profile: Salaried professionals, 25–35, starting SIPs and retirement planning.

  • Heavy mobile usage and short, interruption-prone sessions.
  • Low tolerance for jargon; high need for hand-holding.
  • Prefer quick summaries and “start here” paths.
Segment 02
Engaged portfolio builders

Profile: 30–45, already holding mutual funds or equities.

  • Use the app for quick portfolio checks after market moves.
  • Seek deeper insights, tax optimization, and new opportunities.
  • Expect advanced content without losing clarity.
2–5 minute micro-sessions during commutes or in-between meetings.
“Is everything okay?” portfolio checks after market or news events.

Insight 01
Beginner labels were invisible

13 of 20 participants used the first “Skill up” card but 7 completely missed the small “Beginner” label until asked. Tiny, low-contrast meta-tags failed in real micro-sessions.

→ Led to explicit “Start here” sections & prominent level badges.

Insight 02
Users wanted portfolio-linked insights

15 participants asked for news and tips directly connected to their holdings instead of generic market headlines. “Because you invest in equity funds…” resonated strongly.

→ Informed the personalized “For you” stream and dashboard tiles.

Insight 03
Gamification looked like advertising

8 participants scrolled past “Play & Learn” assuming it was a promo. Others worried about whether real money was at risk, eroding trust before any interaction.

→ Drove clearer framing: “Practice with virtual money, no impact on portfolio.”

Structure

Information architecture & flows

The IA intentionally connects education and action: insights are always one or two taps away from products, and vice versa.

Insights & Knowledge Center

Entry points from global navigation and contextual links:

  • “Insights & Knowledge” in the main nav.
  • “Learn more about ETFs” from relevant dashboard tiles.
  • Notifications like “Market update for your mutual funds”.

Primary tabs:

  • Articles, News, Videos, and a personalized Insights stream.
  • Cross-level filters for Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced.

Secondary features keep users engaged without feeling sold to:

  • “Play & Learn” virtual portfolio game as a safe practice space.
  • “Today’s Expert Speak” spotlight for high-signal video advice.
  • Saved/bookmarked items across all media types.

Core flows:

  • Browse → Filter by media/level → Read → Save/Share → Back to hub.
  • Dashboard notification → Deep-linked article → Contextual CTA (e.g., “Review SIPs”).

Customer dashboard

From static summary to a decision surface

The dashboard is structured as: hero overview (net worth, allocation, trend), goal-centric “Explore promises”, financial fitness, curated insights, products, and services like advisor connect and payments.

Each key metric intentionally links to one or two recommended actions—improve score, rebalance, protect risk—so users never hit a dead end of just “numbers”.

Next

What I would do next

With more time and access to production data, the next phase would focus on sharpening personalization, simplifying high-frequency tasks, and closing the loop between learning and investing.

A. Strengthen the Insights hub

  • Add a “Start here” strip with 3–5 tiles like “Investing basics” and “Mutual funds 101”.
  • Introduce a “For you” tab biased to the user’s holdings and risk profile.
  • Expose level filters (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced) across all media types.
  • Clarify “Play & Learn” as “Practice investing with virtual money, no real risk”.
  • Track learning progress with badges tied to real concepts, not vanity points.

B. Evolve the dashboard

  • Add a “What matters now” row showing SIPs due, credit score deltas, or rebalance nudges.
  • Group content into sticky sub-nav sections: Overview, Goals, Insights, Products, Payments.
  • Let users collapse sections and persist those preferences across sessions.
  • Elevate “Easy payments” or offer a quick “Investments / Payments” toggle for frequent tasks.
  • Design explicit empty states for no investments, pending KYC, or very low balances.

C. Measure & iterate pragmatically

  • Track content completion rates, time-on-insights, and save/share behavior by level.
  • Instrument flows from content to product exploration and new investments.
  • Run A/B tests on CTA copy, card layouts, and dashboard ordering to tune for clarity.
  • Use quarterly diary studies to understand how perceived confidence shifts over time.

Target outcomes

  • Increase content engagement in the Insights hub by ~20%.
  • Improve cross-sell from dashboard recommendations by ~5%.
  • Boost self-reported investment confidence for first-time investors by ~30%.
  • Reduce dependency on advisor calls for basic decisions by ~15%.
Reflection

Key learnings

This project reinforced core principles that carry across complex, high-stakes domains like healthcare and fintech.

Trust is the primary UI

Even subtle sales language or ambiguous rewards can erode trust. Clear sourcing, disclaimers, and intent labelling must be first-class design elements.

Education works best in context

Users rarely visit standalone “learning centers”. Embedding guidance at the exact moment of decision drives far better uptake.

Progressive disclosure beats “all at once”

Financial data is heavy by default. Starting simple and revealing depth on demand lowers anxiety without blocking expert users.

Design for goals, not products

Organizing around life goals (home, retirement, education) aligns with how people think, making complex product choices feel more human and relatable.
