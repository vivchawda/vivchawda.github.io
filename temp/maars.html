Background

The Challenge of Trust and Tech

Smallholder farmers form the backbone of agriculture in India, yet they are largely excluded from modern financial ecosystems. MAARS was conceived to bridge this gap, providing a secure platform to buy agricultural inputs and access micro-financing.

The Contextual Barriers

  • ⚠ Low Digital Literacy: Standard text-heavy UIs caused instant cognitive overload.
  • ⚠ Shared Devices: Phones are passed around families and villages, breaking standard authentication models.
  • ⚠ Spotty Connectivity: Transactions frequently failed due to dropping from 3G to Edge mid-field.

Core Challenge

To design for this demographic, we couldn't rely on standard Silicon Valley UX patterns. Every interaction had to be localized, visual, and highly forgiving. The UI needed to build absolute trust with users who were deeply skeptical of digital banking.

Methodology

Co-creation over Assumption

An immersive, on-field design process prioritizing direct interaction with the end-users in rural environments.

1

Contextual Inquiry

Field Visits · Ethnographic Research

Traveled to rural agricultural hubs in India to observe how farmers currently managed finances, crop sales, and mobile usage. Discovered the prevalence of shared village devices.

2

Co-Creation Workshops

Paper Prototyping · Iconography Testing

Conducted workshops directly with farmer groups. Tested physical cards with different icon styles to determine which visual metaphors were universally understood regardless of local dialect.

3

Interaction Design

Visual PINs · Voice Prompts · Offline Sync

Designed low-literacy interaction patterns. Replaced complex alphanumeric passwords with visual PIN pads and color-coded confirmation screens to overcome language barriers.

4

Harsh-Environment Testing

Sunlight Legibility · 2G Latency

Field tested high-fidelity prototypes under direct, glaring sunlight on low-brightness budget Android devices. Simulated network drops to ensure data persistence during transaction flows.

Definition Phase

Architecting Trust

1. Visual Over Text

Iconography and color-coding must drive the primary narrative. Text acts only as secondary support for those who can read it.

2. Forgiving Security

Security cannot equal friction. We must protect shared devices using visual PINs and SMS OTPs rather than complex typed passwords.

3. Resilient Workflows

The UI must gracefully handle network drops, saving state locally and confirming visually when data syncs successfully.

The Voice Assistance Layer

A major breakthrough during prototyping was the introduction of a Voice Assistance toggle. When users performed critical actions (like transferring money), a localized audio prompt would read the transaction details aloud. This multi-sensory feedback loop dramatically increased user confidence and directly contributed to the spike in successful task completions.

Results

Impact at Scale

By treating extreme environmental constraints—low literacy, shared devices, and 2G connectivity—as our primary design drivers, we successfully launched MAARS to the Google Play Store.

Iterative field testing led to a 55% improvement in task success rates for critical flows like onboarding and purchasing. The platform rapidly scaled to serve over 10,000 farmers and was recognized under McKinsey's United Nations Design for Good initiative, directly driving financial inclusion aligned with UN Sustainable Development Goals.
