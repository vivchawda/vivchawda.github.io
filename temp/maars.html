MAARS: Financial inclusion for 10,000+ rural farmers.

Designing a secure mobile commerce platform that overcomes the barriers of low digital literacy, spotty connectivity, and shared devices in rural India.

Role Associate Design Director
Domain Agri-Fintech / B2B2C
Platform Android (Low-End Devices)
Impact UN Design for Good Aligned
The Context

The Challenge of Trust and Technology

Smallholder farmers form the backbone of agriculture in India, yet they are largely excluded from modern financial and commerce ecosystems. The MAARS initiative was conceived to bridge this gap by providing a platform to buy agricultural inputs, sell crops securely, and access micro-financing.

However, launching a fintech app in this demographic presents severe contextual challenges. Users have low digital literacy, internet connectivity drops frequently, and smartphones are often shared among multiple family members or village heads.

Field Research

Co-creation over Assumption

To design for this demographic, we couldn't rely on standard Silicon Valley UX patterns. We went directly into the field, conducting co-creation workshops and usability tests with farmer groups in rural India.

The "Shared Device" Reality

A single Android phone is often passed around a family or a village. This required us to rethink session management and authentication entirely. Auto-login was a security risk, but typing complex passwords caused user drop-off.

Linguistic and Visual Literacy

Text-heavy interfaces caused immediate cognitive overload. Users responded significantly better to localized iconography, color-coded status indicators, and voice-assisted prompts in regional dialects.

Intermittent Connectivity

Transactions often failed due to network drops in the middle of a field. The UI needed to reassure users, preserve data locally, and automatically resume sync when the connection was restored.

Design Execution

Architecting Trust

Security Without Friction

To handle the shared-device problem, I designed a multi-tiered authentication flow. We utilized SMS-based OTPs (One Time Passwords) combined with a simple visual PIN pad. This eliminated the need for complex alphanumeric passwords while securing financial data.

Visual Information Architecture

We replaced standard data tables with card-based layouts using high-contrast iconography. Crop prices, weather alerts, and transaction histories were color-coded, allowing users to gauge status at a glance under harsh sunlight on low-brightness screens.

Outcomes

Impact at Scale

By treating the environmental and cognitive constraints as primary design drivers rather than edge cases, we successfully launched MAARS to the Google Play Store.

The iterative testing and refinement led to a 55% improvement in task success rates for critical flows like onboarding and completing a purchase. The project successfully scaled to serve over 10,000 farmers and was recognized as part of McKinsey's United Nations Design for Good initiative, directly aligning with UN Sustainable Development Goals.
